A multicurrency nominated margin account
Multicurrency based margin accounts allow brokers to minimize volatile risks between clients’ equity and brokers’ equity. Margin accounts can be nominated in any currency from B2Broker’s liquidity, including cryptocurrencies. Client accounts in different currencies which are correlated to each other can be connected to one margin account. BNB and BTC based accounts can easily work with a BTC based margin account with minimal risk on volatility differences between the two currencies, for example.
Without taking a risk
The example above contains a complete diversification for the base currencies of customer groups, according to margin accounts based on the same base currencies. In this case, brokers will work with the same amount of capital as their clients, without risking volatility for each currency.
Extended risk management
In a multicurrency nominated margin account model the broker needs to control the equity on all his margin accounts in order to provide execution for all his clients. This means the broker has to keep more funds - close to 100% of client funds, on his margin accounts to avoid rejections for client orders due to insufficient funds.
Single Margin Accounts
The traditional scheme of brokerage with the use of a single margin account, based on the most popular clients’ base currency. This scheme works with small volatility risks on basic fiat currencies, but has a huge volatility risk with crypto and exotic currencies.
Full Netting Risk Management
The example above contains one margin account based in US Dollars, and a lot of client groups based in different currencies. In this case, brokers will operate with US dollars only, but their clients have diversified portfolios which drive the broker to take a risk from the volatility of clients' base currencies.
Volatility Risk
If one client deposits with 10 BTC, the broker will cover the equity of the client by the current rate of 5000 USD per BTC = 50000 USD on the brokers' margin account. If the client makes a profit equal to 1 BTC, the broker will receive profit by the current rate of 7000 USD per BTC = 7000 USD. As a result, clients’ equity = 11 BTC by the rate of 7000 USD per BTC, equal to 77000 USD, and the broker has 57000 USD equity to cover the client. By the rate of 10000 USD per BTC and above, this difference will go up significantly.

FAQs
Have a question? A source of useful information about our products and services.
Why do I need to place deposit?
The deposit on Margin Account is using for trades confirmation of all connected trading sessions. It could be trading servers, like Metatrader, or other FIX API sessions. Also, deposited funds are using to provide trading operations on the Margin Account.
Can I get datafeed only from you?
The liquidity could be used as a Datafeed, and a trading session could be disabled on the bridge/gateway level. At any moment the trading session could be activated back to cover necessary risks.
Where do you take liquidity from?
Our liquidity consists of an aggregated flow of liquidity from tier-one counterparties, which allows us to provide minimum levels of spreads and commissions.
What is the Margin Call and Stop Out level on. your Side?
The Margin Call and Stop Out level are set on 100% and 120% levels of Margin Utilization level, which calculated as (Margin Used / Equity) * 100%.
How do I connect to the liquidity if I have MT4/MT5?
We are providing solutions from PrimeXM and OneZero companies in order to connect Metatrader servers to our liquidity.
Can I use FIX API to connect to the liquidity?
Yes, it is possible to take the liquidity by the FIX API connector.
Does Crypto liquidity available 24/7?
Yes, the Crypto liquidity available 24 by 7, without any holidays.
What is the leverage you provide?
The leverage for FX instruments is 1:100, Margin requiremets for other instruments are available on our Contract Specifications.
Is it possible to put my own markup up on prices of the liquidity stream?
It is possible through your own PrimeXM XCore, OneZero Hub or any other liquidity aggregator/advanced bridge solution.
I need to integrate liquidity ASAP. How quickly you can do this?
The fastest way to connect the liquidity takes 24 hours.
How to avoid Stop Out?
It is recommended to keep the Used margin on the level of 65-75% to avoid Stop Out. Also some positions could be closed in case of too high level of Used Margin.
What happens with the positions in case of Stop Out?
In case of Stop Out positions are automatically closed on PrimeXM/ OneZero level, but remain on MT 4/5 level.
I have a balance discrepancy between MT 4/5 and PrimeXM/OZ after the Stop Out. What should I do?
To avoid balance discrepancy it is recommended follow one from the two options:Option 1. Deposit money to the marginal account Deposit necessary amount of money on marginal account on PrimeXM / OneZero to provide enough margin for the positions Re-open positions on PrimeXM / OneZero Option 2. Close positions in MT 4 / 5How to close the positions in A-Book on MT levelTo avoid any discrepancy between marginal account and MT, you should close the positions which were closed on PrimeXM / OZ also in MT following the steps below:Move the accounts with the positions to close from A-Book group to the B-Book group.IMPORTANT: Make sure that the following parameters in the group from which you are moving accounts and the group to which you are moving accounts are the same:Mark-upCurrencySystem: Hedging / Netting Close the positionsMove the accounts back to the A-Book group if needed.
What is the rollover?
Rollover is the process when the positions are shifted to the next trading day and SWAPs are calculated. Rollover is proceed automatically every day at the fixed same moment of time.
How the rollover can influence the opened positions?
During the rollover often spread can be expanded, so some positions can be closed by Stop Out in can of the too bog level of the Used Margin. So it is recommended to keep the level of the Used Margin on the level of 65-75%.
Why the position was closed / limit order opened while the chart displays the price haven't reached the defined level?
